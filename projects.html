Enhancing Visual Quality of Images with Improved GAN and CNN Models

An increasing number of users are becoming interested in the automatic image coloring approach, which has applications in a variety of fields, and involve restoring old or damaged image to an enhance form, typically it dwells on improving grayscale image or poor quality color image to a better quality image. Unfortunately, the current applications for automating image coloring have some number of shortcomings, on the one hand, this is primarily due to a lack of operational resources for the applications, and on the other hand, it is due to the inaccuracies of the coloring techniques. In order to addressed these issues, this research proposed an enhanced automatic image colorization approach capable of improving poor quality image and recovering damage images in order to improves its visual quality to a greater extend. The research adopted a Generative Adversarial Network (GAN) and Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) models for analysis. Datasets from Kaggle were used as a benchmark dataset, with many of the most recent advancements in automatic image coloring studies adopting them as a starting point. The model was trained with the help of the datasets. The findings indicate that there is a reasonable degree of accuracy in recovering an old or damaged image and that the visual quality of the image improved significantly


Abstract There is growing interest in automatic image colorization, which can enhance old or damaged images by converting grayscale or poor-quality images into better quality ones. Current automatic colorization methods have limitations due to insufficient resources and inaccuracies in techniques. To address these issues, this research introduces an improved automatic image colorization method that enhances the quality of poor or damaged images. This method uses advanced Generative Adversarial Networks (GAN) and Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN). We used datasets from Kaggle for training the model. The findings show that our approach significantly improves the visual quality of old or damaged images, providing a higher degree of accuracy and better results than previous methods.
